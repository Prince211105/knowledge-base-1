# WBS decomposition process

## Overview

Describes how stakeholders decompose a WBS from major deliverables into more detailed deliverable components.

## Detailed Summary

WBS decomposition begins by identifying the top-level major deliverable components to be produced by the project. Stakeholders then drill down through each higher-level parent deliverable, adding more detailed components that make up each parent. Decomposition continues while maintaining focus on deliverables rather than activities, and while checking completeness and adherence to the 100% rule.

## Content

To decompose a WBS, stakeholders first identify the major deliverable categories at the top of the hierarchy. They then examine each parent deliverable and ask what lower-level deliverables make it up. This drilling down continues until the team has sufficient clarity about what will be produced. The process is collaborative and focused on deliverables, not tasks. During decomposition, the team checks whether anything required by the project solution is missing or whether any out-of-scope item has been included. Decomposition naturally ends when further breakdown can only be expressed as activities rather than deliverables.

## Best Practices

- Decompose collaboratively with stakeholders who understand the solution.
- Pause at each level to validate completeness and scope boundaries.

## Examples

- In a mountain bike WBS, the team starts with major components and drills into subcomponents of the frame set, wheels, and other deliverables.

## Warnings

- If decomposition turns into a list of work actions, the team has crossed into scheduling.

## Limitations

- Decomposition quality depends on stakeholder knowledge of the intended solution.

## Troubleshooting

- If a parent deliverable remains vague, continue decomposition until the output is understandable.
- If decomposition produces verbs, stop and transition to activity planning.

## Decision Rules

- Keep decomposing while additional deliverable nouns add meaningful clarity.
- Stop WBS decomposition when the next breakdown can only be expressed as verb phrases describing production steps.
