# Step 7 – Share the Project Initiation Document

## Overview

Distribute the PID to the entire team, onboard new members, obtain client sign‑off, and use it as a reference for future check‑ins.

## Detailed Summary

The final step is to disseminate the completed PID to all relevant stakeholders. The document should be shared with the internal team, any new members joining later, and the client for formal sign‑off, which captures agreed assumptions, RACI assignments, and other critical information. Making the PID easily accessible supports ongoing check‑ins, helps keep the project on track, and serves as a reference point when new stakeholders are introduced or when approval processes become more complex than anticipated.

## Content

It’s done, you’ve created a comprehensive Project Initiation Document that sets your team up for success. Make sure to share it with the entire team and to new team members that are joining later on. It’s also great for check‑ins, later on, to ensure the team doesn’t lose track. Have the client sign off on it as well to formalize things like assumptions and RACI. It’ll come in handy to point to this document when new stakeholders get involved or when approval process turns out to be more complicated than anticipated.

## Best Practices

- Store the PID in a shared, version‑controlled location.
- Notify stakeholders via email with a link to the PID.
