# What information belongs in other project deliverables instead of the WBS

## Overview

Clarifies which planning details belong in schedule, network diagrams, and budget rather than in the WBS.

## Detailed Summary

Although WBS deliverables are eventually expanded into activities, durations, resources, and costs required to produce them, those pieces of information are not part of the WBS. They belong in other project deliverables, such as the Project Schedule, Network Diagrams, and Budget, which are based on the WBS.

## Content

The WBS remains focused on deliverables even though other planning artifacts rely on it. Activity definitions, durations, resources, sequencing logic, and cost estimates are all necessary to execute and control the project, but they belong in companion documents rather than in the WBS itself. This distinction helps maintain clean scope definition and traceability across planning outputs.

## Can Answer

- Which artifact should contain activities, dependencies, resources, and costs
- How the WBS relates to other planning outputs

## Best Practices

- Maintain a clear separation between scope definition and detailed planning data.
- Use the WBS as the common source for downstream planning artifacts.

## Examples

- Activities go in the Project Schedule, dependencies in Network Diagrams, and costs in the Budget.

## Warnings

- Mixing all planning data into the WBS weakens clarity and maintainability.

## Limitations

- The WBS alone is not sufficient for execution control without the related artifacts.

## Troubleshooting

- If teams are unsure where data belongs, categorize it by question: what equals WBS, how and when equals schedule, cost equals budget, sequence equals network diagrams.

## Decision Rules

- Put deliverables in the WBS and operational planning data in the project schedule, network diagrams, and budget.
