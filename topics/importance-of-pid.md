# Why a Project Initiation Document (PID) Is Essential

A solid start is essential for any project, regardless of size. The PID guides the team early, reduces extra work later, and acts as a safeguard when resources change or new members join. It sets tone, context, expectations, and constraints, helping to prevent projects from going off‑track due to unclear objectives or missing information.

## Can Answer

- Why should I create a PID?
- What problems does a PID prevent?

## Examples

- A team that started without a PID missed critical stakeholder approvals, leading to rework.

## Warnings

- Skipping the PID can result in misaligned expectations and project delays.
