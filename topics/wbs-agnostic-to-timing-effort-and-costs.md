# WBS agnostic to timing, effort, and costs

## Overview

Explains that the WBS does not encode schedule, effort, or cost information.

## Detailed Summary

The WBS only represents what needs to be produced as a result of the project. It is agnostic to timing, effort, and costs. Durations and resource assignments belong in the Project Schedule, and costs belong in the Project Budget. This separation is essential because the WBS functions as the blueprint and justification for schedule and budget rather than containing those details directly.

## Content

A key characteristic of the WBS is neutrality regarding time, effort, and cost. It should not embed deadlines, durations, labor assumptions, or monetary values. Those details are developed later after the team has clarified the required deliverables. By staying agnostic, the WBS remains a clean scope artifact and can serve as the basis for objective planning and change control.

## Best Practices

- Keep scope definition separate from estimation and sequencing.
- Use the WBS to justify estimates rather than to store them.

## Examples

- A deliverable such as Budget Release Schedule may appear as a deliverable, but numeric budget estimates do not belong in the WBS itself.

## Warnings

- Adding time or cost details to the WBS can create false precision before scope is fully agreed.

## Limitations

- A timing-agnostic WBS cannot by itself support execution tracking without linked schedule artifacts.

## Troubleshooting

- If stakeholders request dates or costs in the WBS, explain that those details belong in schedule and budget documents derived from the WBS.

## Decision Rules

- Retain only deliverable descriptions in the WBS; move numeric time, effort, and cost data to other project artifacts.
