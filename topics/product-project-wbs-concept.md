# Product project WBS concept

## Overview

Describes how a WBS applies to projects that produce a product.

## Detailed Summary

A project that produces a product will have a WBS documenting all components of the product. Examples mentioned include creating a bicycle, software application, building structure, automobile, or training class. The WBS captures the product deliverables and their components rather than the activities needed to build them.

## Content

For product-oriented projects, the WBS represents the components of the end product. The document cites examples such as bicycles, software applications, buildings, automobiles, and training classes. The purpose is to define everything the product solution must include so that the project can later derive activities, costs, and schedules from that agreed deliverable structure.

## Can Answer

- How product outcomes appear in a WBS

## Best Practices

- Focus on product components and supporting deliverables rather than build steps.
- Use enough decomposition to understand what the product includes.

## Examples

- A bicycle project WBS would document bike components.
- A training class project WBS would document course deliverables.

## Warnings

- Do not confuse product components with the tasks used to assemble or create them.

## Limitations

- Complex product elements may require deeper decomposition than simple procured components.

## Troubleshooting

- If the WBS for a product project becomes a manufacturing procedure, reframe the items as product outputs.

## Decision Rules

- For product projects, represent the physical or defined product components as deliverables in the WBS.
