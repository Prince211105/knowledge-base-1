# Why a Project Initiation Document (PID) Is Essential

A PID sets the tone and context for a project, clarifies mission and success criteria, and records decisions up front. By documenting scope, objectives, constraints, and risk mitigation strategies, it aligns the team and stakeholders, supports onboarding of new members, and serves as a reference when resources or requirements change. This reduces miscommunication, scope creep, and off‑track progress, and facilitates smoother hand‑overs and stakeholder sign‑offs throughout the project lifecycle.
