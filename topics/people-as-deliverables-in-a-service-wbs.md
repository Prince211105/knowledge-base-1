# People as deliverables in a service WBS

## Overview

Explains when people can appear as deliverables in the WBS.

## Detailed Summary

Deliverable categories may include people when those people are part of the service solution being provided. In the wedding planning example, the Ceremony branch includes a deliverable called Minister because a minister may be needed to perform the wedding for the customer. If this deliverable is left off the WBS, the activities for engaging that person may be missed in planning and budgeting until the last minute.

## Content

Although the WBS normally focuses on outputs, service solutions may include specific people as deliverable elements when those people are part of what the customer receives. In such cases, the person is represented because their inclusion is necessary to define the service scope. Omitting such a person from the WBS can cause the associated work and cost to be overlooked until late in the project.

## Can Answer

- Whether people can be WBS deliverables
- Why service personnel may belong in the WBS
- How omission affects planning

## Best Practices

- Include service personnel in the WBS when they are part of the agreed service solution.
- Check whether omission of a person would cause planning gaps.

## Examples

- Minister appears as a deliverable under Ceremony in the wedding planning service WBS.

## Warnings

- Leaving a key person-based deliverable off the WBS can trigger late schedule and cost change requests.

## Limitations

- Not every project role belongs in the WBS; only those that are part of the service solution being provided.

## Troubleshooting

- If a critical service engagement was overlooked, verify whether the person or role should have appeared as a deliverable in the WBS.

## Decision Rules

- Include a person in the WBS only when that person is part of the service solution the customer is receiving.
