# High-level WBS for initial rough order of magnitude estimates

## Overview

Explains how a high-level WBS can support early ROM schedule and budget planning.

## Detailed Summary

A final version of the WBS is not required before producing an initial schedule or budget. A high-level WBS developed early in scope elaboration can provide a high-level understanding of deliverables that is often sufficient to substantiate a rough order of magnitude (ROM) schedule and budget. The focus should remain on deliverables, not activities.

## Content

Projects often need early schedule and budget estimates before the WBS is fully decomposed. In such cases, a high-level WBS can provide enough scope definition to support ROM planning. This early WBS should still be deliverables-based and should document the major outputs expected from the project. As the WBS is refined, the schedule and budget can be refined as well.

## Can Answer

- Whether a full WBS is required for early estimates
- How a high-level WBS supports ROM estimates
- How to keep early WBS planning focused

## Best Practices

- Create a high-level WBS as early as possible in scope elaboration.
- Explicitly label early estimates as rough order of magnitude when based on high-level deliverables only.

## Examples

- A top-level mountain bike WBS can support an initial ROM schedule and budget even before every subcomponent is decomposed.

## Warnings

- Do not treat ROM figures derived from a high-level WBS as fully detailed commitments.

## Limitations

- A high-level WBS cannot justify fine-grained schedule logic or detailed cost allocations.

## Troubleshooting

- If early estimates lack credibility, verify that major deliverable categories are captured in the high-level WBS.

## Decision Rules

- Use a high-level WBS for ROM estimates when detailed decomposition is not yet complete.
