# Service project WBS concept

## Overview

Describes how a WBS applies to projects whose outcome is a service.

## Detailed Summary

A service project will have a WBS documenting all components associated with the service offering a customer can experience. Examples include wedding planning services, cleaning services, audit services, and repair services. The WBS can act as a checklist of deliverables used to configure the service and can include interim deliverables associated with providing the service.

## Content

Service projects still produce deliverables, even when the final outcome is experiential rather than physical. In such projects, the WBS documents service components and associated outputs that must be prepared or delivered to the customer. The document notes that these can function as a checklist of service-related deliverables and can include tangible outputs or interim deliverables needed to provide the service effectively.

## Best Practices

- Treat service components and configuration outputs as deliverables.
- Use the WBS as a checklist to prevent service elements from being overlooked.

## Examples

- A wedding planning service WBS can include Ceremony, Reception, and Transportation deliverables.

## Warnings

- Assuming a service has no deliverables can create planning and budgeting omissions.

## Limitations

- Service deliverables may be less visually tangible than product components and require careful wording.

## Troubleshooting

- If stakeholders struggle to define service deliverables, ask what the customer will receive, experience, or rely on.

## Decision Rules

- If a service component or associated output is needed to provide the service, include it in the WBS.
