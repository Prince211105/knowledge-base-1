# WBS as the agreed scope baseline

## Overview

Explains how a baselined WBS establishes agreement on what will be built.

## Detailed Summary

Once the project WBS is baselined, it establishes agreement on the scope of what will be built. It becomes the focal point for defining schedulable activities and supports objective scope decisions. This baseline reflects stakeholder alignment on all in-scope deliverables before significant effort is spent on design and build.

## Content

Baselining the WBS means stakeholders have agreed on the deliverables the project will produce. That agreement creates a stable scope reference for activity definition, planning justification, and change control. Because the WBS captures the "what" of the project, it serves as the baseline against which additions, removals, and scope creep can be evaluated objectively.

## Best Practices

- Seek stakeholder agreement before treating the WBS as the planning baseline.
- Use the baseline consistently for change evaluation.

## Examples

- A baselined WBS can be used to decide whether a proposed deliverable is scope creep or approved scope.

## Warnings

- Using an unagreed WBS as a planning baseline can destabilize schedule and budget assumptions.

## Limitations

- A baseline still needs approved updates when scope changes are accepted.

## Troubleshooting

- If teams disagree about whether work is in scope, compare the work to the baselined WBS deliverables.

## Decision Rules

- Treat the WBS as the formal scope baseline only after stakeholder agreement has been established.
