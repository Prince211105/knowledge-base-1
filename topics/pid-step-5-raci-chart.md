# Step 5 – Define Who’s Who (RACI Chart) in a PID

## Overview

Identify roles and responsibilities using a RACI matrix to clarify who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for each deliverable.

## Detailed Summary

Step 5 of the PID creation process documents the project team structure and decision‑making hierarchy. A RACI chart is a common tool for this purpose. The chart lists project deliverables or activities on one axis and roles (both internal and external) on the other axis, assigning one of four responsibility designations: Responsible (who does the work), Accountable (who makes the final decision), Consulted (who must be consulted before proceeding), and Informed (who receives updates). Only one person should be accountable per deliverable to avoid confusion. The RACI matrix helps set expectations for communication, governance, and sign‑off procedures, reducing the risk of “I thought that was on your plate” misunderstandings.

## Content

An important aspect of the Project Initiation Document is the overall project team structure both internally and externally. Who is working on the team? Who can approve things before they go to a client? Who on the client side needs to be consulted before a sign off?

A great way to indicate these dependencies in the project is a RACI chart. It outlines who is / needs to be:
Responsible – Who gets the job done?
Accountable – Who is the decision maker?
Consulted – Who needs to be asked before proceeding?
Informed – Who needs to be kept in the loop?

The best way to start a RACI is to map out the project deliverables or activities on the left hand side and the Roles on the right hand side – both for internal and external team members and then assign the RACI status. Make sure there is only one person accountable per deliverable.

## Best Practices

- Ensure only one person is marked Accountable per deliverable.
- Include both internal and external stakeholders in the RACI.
