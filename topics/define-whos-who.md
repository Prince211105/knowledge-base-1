# Create a RACI Chart to Define Project Roles

A RACI chart clarifies responsibilities for each deliverable or activity: Responsible (who does the work), Accountable (who makes final decisions), Consulted (who provides input), Informed (who needs updates). List project activities on the left and roles (internal and external) across the top, then assign RACI letters. Ensure only one person is Accountable per activity to avoid decision bottlenecks.

## Can Answer

- How do I create a RACI chart for my project?
- What do the RACI letters stand for?
- How should I assign the Accountable role in a RACI chart?

## Examples

- Activity: Final Design Review – Responsible: Designer, Accountable: Creative Director, Consulted: UX Lead, Informed: Client PM.

## Warnings

- Assigning multiple Accountable persons creates confusion during approvals.
