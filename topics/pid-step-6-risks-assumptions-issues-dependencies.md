# Step 6 – Identify Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies

## Overview

Document known risks, assumptions, potential issues, and dependencies, along with mitigation strategies, to proactively manage uncertainties.

## Detailed Summary

The sixth step in creating a PID is to capture any known risks, assumptions, issues, and dependencies that could impact the project. Risks may include timeline constraints, budget limitations, technical unknowns, a complex stakeholder landscape, or single points of failure. Assumptions are conditions taken for granted that could affect outcomes if they prove false. Issues are current problems that need resolution, while dependencies are external or internal factors that the project relies upon. For each identified item, include a brief mitigation or resolution strategy to reduce its impact.

## Content

Last but not least, make sure to include an overview of known risks and constraints into the PID. Projects can be complex for different reasons and it’s always helpful to think it through and anticipate some of the project risks and issues and come up with mitigation strategies.

Some examples are:
Timelines that are too short or too long
Budget constraints
Technical unknowns
Complex Stakeholder landscape
Single point of failures

- Provide a brief mitigation plan for each identified risk.
- Review and update the risk list as the project progresses.
