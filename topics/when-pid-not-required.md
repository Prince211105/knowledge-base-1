# When a Project Initiation Document (PID) Can Be Omitted

For very small, low‑risk initiatives (e.g., a single‑day design tweak) a lightweight brief may suffice. However, even in small projects, capturing at least context and deliverables can prevent misunderstand‑ings.

## Can Answer

- When is it acceptable to skip a PID?
- What minimal documentation is appropriate for tiny tasks?

## Examples

- Updating a CSS color palette for an existing site – a short email brief can replace a full PID.

## Warnings

- Even small tasks can benefit from a brief; skipping documentation entirely may cause loss of context.
