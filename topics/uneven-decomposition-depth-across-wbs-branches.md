# Uneven decomposition depth across WBS branches

## Overview

Explains why some WBS branches are decomposed deeper than others and what that can indicate.

## Detailed Summary

In the workplace relocation example, some level 2 deliverables have been decomposed to level 3 while others have not. This is common during brainstorming because more complex or troublesome deliverables often come to mind early. The example suggests that the WBS is likely not fully decomposed because several level 2 deliverables remain too subjective, such as Packing Materials, Utilities, Transition Plan, and Supplies.

## Content

WBS branches do not always evolve uniformly. During brainstorming, the team may expand certain complex or risky branches sooner than others. This uneven depth is normal, but it can also indicate that the WBS is incomplete if some branches remain too vague. Subjective or broad deliverables usually need further decomposition before the WBS can be considered sufficiently clear.

## Can Answer

- Whether all WBS branches require equal depth
- Why brainstorming causes uneven branch detail
- How to spot branches needing more work

## Best Practices

- Accept uneven depth during early brainstorming, then revisit shallow subjective branches.
- Use clarity rather than symmetry as the criterion for decomposition depth.

## Examples

- Packing Materials, Utilities, Transition Plan, and Supplies are examples of level 2 items that may still be too subjective and need more detail.

## Warnings

- Assuming a WBS is complete just because some branches are detailed can hide incomplete scope elsewhere.

## Limitations

- Depth variation alone does not prove incompleteness; the key issue is whether each branch is sufficiently clear.

## Troubleshooting

- If a branch remains broad or subjective, continue decomposing it until stakeholders understand what will be produced.

## Decision Rules

- Do not force equal depth across all branches; decompose each branch to the level needed for clarity.
- If a deliverable remains subjective, decompose further.
