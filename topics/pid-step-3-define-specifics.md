# Step 3 – Define the Specifics in a PID

## Overview

This section clarifies what is in scope, out of scope, initial requirements, boundaries, and required deliverables, including assumptions and constraints.

## Detailed Summary

Step 3 of the PID creation process focuses on the specifics of what the project will deliver. The project manager must list what is in scope and what is out of scope, identify any initial requirements already defined, and state the boundaries that the team must not cross. This section also details the required deliverables, expectations around revisions, assumptions, and any constraints such as regulatory limits or technology restrictions. By clearly defining these specifics, the team knows exactly what needs to be built and delivered, reducing ambiguity and preventing scope creep.

## Content

What is in scope and out of scope?
Are there some initial project requirements that are already defined?
What are the project boundaries that the team shall not cross?

A crucial part of this section is the required deliverables. Since these are typically defined early on in the contract, the team needs to understand what the expectations are. This includes potential assumptions and constraints, like the number of revisions to consider.

## Best Practices

- Clearly separate in‑scope and out‑of‑scope items using bullet lists.
- Document any assumptions that affect deliverable expectations.
