# WBS as the foundation for schedule, budget, sequencing, and scope control

## Overview

Describes how the WBS supports downstream planning and scope management.

## Detailed Summary

The WBS establishes the foundation stakeholders use for creating the project schedule, building network diagrams to sequence work, preparing budgets for estimating and managing costs, and making objective decisions about scope creep. It is foundational because it encompasses every deliverable for the final product, service, or process, and may also include intermediary methodology outcomes required to manage the project effort.

## Content

The WBS is the base planning artifact from which key project management deliverables are derived. Once the project team agrees on the WBS, stakeholders can define activities, create network diagrams, prepare schedules, estimate budgets, and evaluate change requests against an agreed scope baseline. The WBS is also used to objectively manage scope creep because it defines what is in scope and what is not. Since it captures every required deliverable, it provides the justification for schedule and budget assumptions.

## Can Answer

- Why the WBS comes before schedule and budget
- How the WBS supports objective scope decisions
- How the WBS helps sequence and cost work indirectly

## Best Practices

- Baseline the WBS before detailed scheduling and budgeting.
- Use the WBS as the reference point for scope change evaluation.

## Examples

- A team can compare a proposed scope change to the approved WBS and decide whether a new deliverable should be added.

## Warnings

- Skipping the WBS weakens the justification for schedule and budget assumptions.

## Limitations

- The WBS supports planning but does not replace schedule, network, or budget documents.

## Troubleshooting

- If schedule or budget assumptions are hard to explain, verify whether the corresponding deliverables are clearly represented in the WBS.

## Decision Rules

- If planning decisions require confirmation of project scope, consult the approved WBS.
- If a change introduces a new output, update the WBS before adjusting schedule and budget.
