# Step 4 – Define the Project Breakdown Structure and Resourcing Plan

## Overview

Create a hierarchical breakdown of work packages and assign resources to each. This clarifies how deliverables are assembled and who is responsible.

## Detailed Summary

Step 4 involves constructing a Project Breakdown Structure (PBS) that decomposes the overall project into smaller, manageable work packages or activities. Each component is linked to the allocated resources, showing who will perform the work and when. The PBS can be presented at varying levels of detail, often aligning with phases, milestones, or specific dates. By running the team through the PBS during planning, dependencies become visible, and team members can provide input to optimize the plan. The structure also supports resource planning and accountability, ensuring that every deliverable has a clear owner and timeline.

## Content

To ensure it is clear to the team how the deliverables are created, it is essential to break the work into smaller chunks and create a Project Breakdown that includes the allocations. It showcases how deliverables come together and who will be working on what with whom.

High‑level Project Breakdown example:
In my personal experience, it’s of great value to run the team through the overall rough planning and resourcing breakdown structure of the project, so that team members can weigh in and help you optimize. By running the team through the actual planning, dependencies will become clear and the team is part of the plan. It also helps the overall project understanding and accountability. These breakdowns can vary in detail and can map out the various activities in each phase. They can also be connected to specific dates and tie into resource planning.
